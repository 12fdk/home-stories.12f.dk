If you're planning a renovation and searching for an app, you'll encounter at least three names that keep showing up: HomeZada, Houzz Pro, and Home Stories. They all claim to help with home renovation, and on the surface they look like they do the same thing. They don't.

Each one was built for a completely different user, with different problems, different scales, and different assumptions about what you're trying to do. Confusing them is like comparing a golf cart, a pickup truck, and a dedicated cargo bike — they all move things from A to B, but only one is the right tool for the job you're actually doing.

This is a straightforward comparison of the three in 2026. I'll cover what each one does well, where it falls short, and who each one is actually for. If you're a homeowner doing one renovation, the conclusion might surprise you — and the surprising part is probably the right one.

![A renovator standing in a kitchen mid-renovation, comparing three apps on their phone and a tablet](/stock/13.webp)

## HomeZada: the long-game home portfolio manager

HomeZada started as a simple home inventory tool and grew into something much wider: a full home portfolio management platform. You catalog your property room by room, log every appliance and its warranty details, set up maintenance schedules, store documents, and — yes — track renovation projects.

The strength of HomeZada is breadth. It was built for people who think about their home as a long-term asset, not just the next six-month renovation. The built-in maintenance scheduler catches the stuff you forget: filter changes, HVAC servicing, gutter cleaning. The asset registry keeps track of what you own, when you bought it, and what the warranty expires. The property history layer means you can look back and remember exactly which paint colour you used, when the roof was replaced, or what brand of boiler you installed.

That's genuinely useful — but it's also why HomeZada is a bigger app with more screens, more menus, and more things to learn. The renovation module exists *within* a much wider framework. It's not the only thing the app is trying to be.

HomeZada's renovation features include project tracking, budget management, photo uploads, and contractor notes. They work fine for what they are. But they're features *within* a platform whose primary identity is long-term home ownership, not the focused project management you need when the dust is flying and every day matters.

HomeZada also has a free tier, which is generous for its core features. The paid Premium tier unlocks multi-property management, advanced reporting, and deeper integrations. For someone with one home and one renovation, the free tier is probably sufficient. But you're paying for features most people never touch.

**HomeZada is for:** homeowners who own one or more properties and want a running record of everything — maintenance, assets, insurance, renovations. It's a long-game tool. If that's your mindset, it's excellent. If you just want to get one renovation done without the overhead of building a home portfolio, it's more than you need.

## Houzz Pro: the contractor's command centre

Houzz Pro is where HomeZada and Home Stories differ most sharply from each other. Houzz Pro isn't a homeowner tool at all — it's a professional platform designed for contractors, interior designers, architects, and home improvement businesses.

The feature set makes this immediately clear: client management CRM, multi-project dashboards, team assignment and tracking, quote generation and approval workflows, built-in messaging with clients, and a product marketplace where professionals can source materials and earn commissions. It's a business operating system for the home improvement industry.

For a contractor or designer, Houzz Pro is genuinely powerful. It collapses the gap between prospecting, quoting, executing, and delivering — all inside one platform. For a homeowner, most of this is noise. You don't have clients to manage. You don't have a team to assign tasks to. You don't need to generate formal quotes or source products through a marketplace.

The project tracking in Houzz Pro is built for *multiple* projects across *multiple* clients. It handles client communication, task delegation, and progress reporting in ways that are impressive for a business but unnecessary for a single homeowner. The budget tools are designed for contractor billing, not personal renovation budgeting.

That doesn't mean Houzz Pro's project tracking is bad — it's just built for the wrong user. The mental model assumes you're managing five concurrent client projects, not one personal renovation. The UI is optimised for desktop workflows (sending quotes, assigning team tasks), not the on-site phone logging that defines the execution half of a renovation.

Houzz Pro has a free tier for individual professionals, with paid plans unlocking team features, advanced CRM, and deeper analytics. Again, this pricing structure targets businesses, not homeowners.

**Houzz Pro is for:** contractors, designers, and home improvement professionals who manage multiple clients and need a professional-grade project and client management platform. If you're running a renovation business, it's one of the best tools available. If you're a homeowner, it's the wrong tool with too much complexity for your use case.

## Home Stories: focused on one job

Home Stories was built for a different problem than both HomeZada and Houzz Pro. It doesn't try to be a home portfolio manager or a contractor CRM. It tries to be the simplest possible way to track a single renovation from planning through to handover.

The core loop is deliberate: log a cost, add a photo, update a task — all from your phone, all in seconds. The budget structure is pre-configured for renovations: planned cost, actual cost, remaining balance, per-line-item tracking. There's no home inventory module, no maintenance scheduling, no client management. What's there is what you need for one renovation.

That focus creates advantages that both HomeZada and Houzz Pro can't match:

**The on-site experience is the first-class feature.** Home Stories was built for the same reality described in [our comparison with Notion](/blog/notion-for-home-renovation/): you're standing in a dusty room, one-handed, with a contractor waiting for an answer. Every interaction is designed for that context — fast, simple, and repeatable hundreds of times without friction.

**The photo timeline is native, not bolted on.** Home Stories builds a chronological visual record as you log costs and tasks. Every photo is automatically dated and ordered. You get a scrollable before-during-after progression of the build, which is the single most valuable visual record a renovation generates. Neither HomeZada nor Houzz Pro has a native timeline that works this way for homeowners.

**Budget tracking is purpose-built.** The planned-vs-actual-vs-remaining balance is the core concept, pre-configured and always visible. No setup required. No formulas to build. No database schema to design. It just works because it was designed for this one problem from the ground up — the same way [a renovation spreadsheet stops working](/blog/renovation-spreadsheet-alternative/) the moment logging becomes friction-heavy, Home Stories was designed specifically to avoid that failure.

**It's free and fast.** No subscription. No recurring payment. The basic app is completely free on the App Store, with an optional one-time Premium Lifetime upgrade for advanced features. It's a lighter download than both HomeZada and Houzz Pro, and it opens instantly — which matters when you're trying to log something in ten seconds.

Home Stories has one limitation compared to the alternatives: it's narrower in scope. If you need home inventory tracking, maintenance scheduling, or multi-property management, it won't do those things. That's by design.

**Home Stories is for:** homeowners doing one renovation who want a focused, phone-first tool for tracking budget, photos, and tasks without the overhead of a wider platform.

## How they compare side by side

Here's a direct comparison of the three across the features most homeowners care about:

| Feature | HomeZada | Houzz Pro | Home Stories |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary user | Homeowner (portfolio mindset) | Contractor / designer | Homeowner (single renovation) |
| Budget tracking | Yes — basic | Yes — contractor-focused | Yes — purpose-built |
| Photo timeline | Manual uploads | Manual attachments | Automatic, chronological |
| Task management | Basic | Advanced (team) | Simple and focused |
| Contractor notes | Yes | Full CRM | Yes — lightweight |
| On-site mobile experience | Average | Desktop-first | First-class |
| Setup complexity | Moderate | High | Minimal |
| Free plan | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Paid plans | Subscription | Subscription | One-time upgrade |
| Multi-property | Yes | Multi-client | Single project |

The pattern is clear: each tool optimises for the problems of its primary user. HomeZada optimises for long-term home ownership. Houzz Pro optimises for professional project delivery. Home Stories optimises for the reality of one person managing one renovation on a tight budget and timeline.

## Which one should you choose?

The honest answer depends on who you are:

**Use HomeZada if:** you think about your home as a long-term portfolio, you want maintenance scheduling and asset tracking, and you're happy with a broader tool that includes renovation as one module among many. It's the right choice for the homeowner who plans, maintains, and documents across years and possibly multiple properties.

**Use Houzz Pro if:** you're a contractor, designer, or home improvement professional running a business. It's the right choice for anyone who manages multiple client projects and needs a CRM, team tools, and a professional quote workflow. For most homeowners, it's an over-engineered tool.

**Use Home Stories if:** you're one person running one renovation and want a focused, fast, phone-first tool for the daily tracking that most other apps do poorly. It's the right choice for the renovation that happens in real time — with dust, deadlines, and receipts that need to be logged on-site.

Most homeowners don't need the breadth of HomeZada or the business focus of Houzz Pro. They need the focused execution support that Home Stories was built for — and they need it most during the build phase, where [the right tool makes the difference between a controlled budget and a hole you can't fill](/blog/how-to-budget-a-home-renovation/).

## The bottom line

Three tools. Three users. All genuinely useful for their intended audience.

HomeZada is the best tool for homeowners who think in portfolios. Houzz Pro is the best tool for contractors managing multiple clients. Home Stories is the best tool for a homeowner running one renovation with a focus on on-site execution.

Knowing which one is you — not which one has the most features, or the highest download count, or the flashiest marketing — is the only thing that matters. Pick the tool whose primary user is *you*.
